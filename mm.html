MatchMove

Mobile digital payment

MatchMove is one of the world’s fastest growing and most disruptive fintech company in digital payments and next generation banking. In 2015, it’s partnering up with American Express to provide a virtual paying system. The basic premise of the eWallet is to allow customers to shop online with virtual credit cards. At this project, I was leading the first commercial mobile eWallet design on B2B2C business and their customers to spend both online and offline quickly and safely, through instantly issued prepaid card on major card networks. Overall, the goal of this project was to create the first MVP mobile eWallet that empowered customers to P2P fund transfer, Top-up via multiple channels and shop online with a hassle free spending experience.

Role

DISCOVERY


The challenges I met was to understand how the UI & user behaviours integrated with APIs, including of multiple card creation, fund transfer and topup. With the time limited, we would rather pushed the design validation & research till we had the first read-ready prototype. I wanted to start by outlining the user flow how our customers might apply instant prepaid card through their eWallet. I wanted to discover what any potential ideas could be carried to the digital mobile payment experience & UI by combining facial recognition or QR code technology.

I set out on the followings:

  • Worked with the Business Manager to identify the major Personas
  • Worked with the Product Manager to speck out the key use cases
  • Worked with Program manager to outline the workflow based on use cases
  • Brainstormed workflows alongside the Product Manager and developer leads

Design Brief


Problem Statement

After identifying persona and use cases I concluded a design brief for my design thinking and validation.

  • It is hassle process to getting a debit/credit card by opening a checking account in the bank. Most of our customers were students and emigrants who were not able to provide all documents as requested
  • By preventing criminal intent for money laundering activities, KYC document was required some business days on approval. Customers felt annoy to upload ID or fill in a lengthy form or being bombarded by a series of emails during registration
  • There was a pain to store or organise a bunch of physical debit/credit cards in a one place


hypotheses

My hypotheses were that if customers could easily apply or store multiple cards in one place as well as to speed up KYC process combined with QR code or facial recognition technologies, we can ensure the eWallet is easy to use for majority of our customers at MatchMove.



Success Metrics

After agreed with stockholders, they were our success metrics:

  • Boosting the customer’s satisfaction up to 45 % ( rely on a post-survey)
  • Increasing conversation rate per unique user to get a card up to 40 %

UI Solution


Based on the use cases and business requirements, I explored ideas by sketching as many as possible and turned the best concept into a series of mockups. I then validated the mockups internally across the business as MM could give valuable insights. After some iterations and tweaking based on their feedback, I created a final UI which I validated on external customers.

Here is what I concluded for the final design solution:

Applying credit/debit card for most of emigrants or students is a hassle process when you are required to provide financial statements or insurance number. At MVP, I simplify the registration steps, speeding up the way of applying a card by providing a mobile solution. With the following features, I worked on complex problems across the entire wallet experience to make it a truly easy process for our customers. The final features were including:

  • Multiple cards are in one place. Customers can get their card through instantly issued on major card networks at MM
  • Simplifying the KYC steps during registration by implementing with a third party API on identity verification
  • Easy to top up and load cash to card
  • Providing multiple ways to P2P fund transfer through email, SMS, QR code and passcode
  • Gathering all notifications including system and marketing into one app
  • Leveraging on our merchant network by pushing attractive offers to our customers. Encouraging them to spend more
  • Providing an appealing & clean mobile design with MVP features


Impact


I've been working on this project around 3 months and rolled out the first IOS version for investors. At next phase, we validated it with external customers with a series of F2F interviews and prioritised all of the user needs by MoSCoW technique, then also discovered what features would be in the next design iteration, for example, majority of customers did not feel easy to top up from online, also they were expecting to also have a physical card. However, I led the first mobile design version and facilitated all of the validating/usability processes afterwards.
